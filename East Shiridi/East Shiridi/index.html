About The Temple

||ఓం||

This temple is situated near Vuda Park on the Beach Road at Chinna Waltair, East Point Colony in Visakhapatnam.
The Temple Bhoomi Pooja was performed on 19th February 1977.
The Temple was inaugurated on 18th March 1993 by the trust members in the presence of large number of local Sai Devotees.
The Temple opens at 05:15 AM with the Morning Kakad Aarti & will be closed at night after Shej Aarti at 8:30 PM.
Temples dedicated to Marble Idol of Shirdi Saibaba, Vishakeswara (Shiva), Parvathi Devi, Sanishwara, Suryanarayana Swamy, Rama,Hanuman, Ganapathi, Aiyappa, Kumaraswamy, Dattatreya, Sacred Dhuni and Dwarakamai are present in the Temple Complex.

In Hinduism, cows are considered sacred and are highly revered as a symbol of wealth, strength, and motherly affection. They are seen as gentle and nurturing creatures that provide humans with milk, which is considered a divine food in Hinduism.
The importance of cows in Hinduism dates back to ancient times when they were used in religious ceremonies and as a form of currency. In the Vedic scriptures, cows were often mentioned as an essential part of the ritual sacrifices and were considered the most important of all animals.
Hindu scriptures also teach that cows represent the divine and that they should be treated with respect and kindness. Many Hindus believe that taking care of cows and showing them love and compassion brings good karma and spiritual merit.
Furthermore, in Hinduism, the cow is seen as a symbol of non-violence and compassion towards all living beings. This belief has led to the widespread practice of vegetarianism among Hindus, as many consider it wrong to harm or kill any animal, including cows.
Overall, cows are held in high esteem in Hinduism and are considered a significant part of the cultural and spiritual heritage of the religion.
